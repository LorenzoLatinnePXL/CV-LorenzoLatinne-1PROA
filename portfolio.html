Why programming?

Why did I choose to study programming?

I chose to study programming because it opens doors to countless opportunities and empowers me to shape the digital world. It’s a highly in-demand skill with competitive salaries and the flexibility to work across industries or even start my own business. Programming sharpens my problem-solving skills and enables me to transform creative ideas into tangible solutions. I find it exciting to design and build tools that can make tasks easier or even innovate entirely new processes. The sense of accomplishment that comes from solving complex challenges and seeing my creations come to life is incredibly rewarding.

I love that it’s a future-proof skill, keeping me relevant in a tech-driven world that’s constantly changing. The supportive community and wealth of accessible resources make learning both engaging and enjoyable. Programming also offers the chance to contribute to solving global challenges, whether through improving healthcare, advancing sustainability, or enhancing communication. It’s a field that encourages lifelong learning and personal growth, which inspires me to keep pushing my limits and exploring new possibilities.

Why do I think this profession suits me?

A profession in programming suits me well because I thrive on problem-solving and enjoy tackling challenges with a blend of logical thinking and creativity. The process of breaking down complex issues and devising efficient solutions is both stimulating and deeply satisfying for me. I’m passionate about technology and constantly curious about how systems operate, which aligns perfectly with the innovative and ever-evolving nature of programming. Exploring new tools and frameworks excites me, as it opens up endless opportunities to grow and refine my skills.

The flexibility to work remotely and maintain a healthy work-life balance makes this career even more appealing, offering the freedom to excel professionally while dedicating time to personal interests and relationships. This balance is crucial for sustaining motivation and long-term success. I also find great fulfillment in building practical solutions that address real-world needs, whether it’s simplifying tasks, enhancing productivity, or creating something entirely new. Seeing the tangible impact of my work not only inspires me but also reinforces my decision to pursue programming as more than just a career—it’s a meaningful and rewarding path that aligns with my values and aspirations.

Programmer competencies:

Course-specific learning outcomes (OLR)
Associate degree in Programming (PRO)
ROLE DESIGNER
OLR1 The graduate prepares the realization of a software project.
OLR2 Based on the analysis, the graduate makes a substantiated proposal for a) the design, b) the programming language and c) the methodology. The graduate coordinates the proposal with the software developer, analyst and/or project leader.
ROLE PROGRAMMER
OLR3 The graduate realizes software applications and data structures. The graduate works systematically within the context of the project plan, the available tools and the proposed methodology.
OLR4 The graduate is partly responsible for their own digital work environment and contributes to the shared infrastructure required for it developing, testing and putting projects into production.
OLR5 The graduate programs software applications according to the standards and agreements within the organization.
OLR6 The graduate examines, in consultation with the software developer, analyst and/or project leader, whether the delivered product requires maintenance and/or needs adjustments. The graduate carries out maintenance and adjustments in a project-based manner, taking into account with previously made agreements.
ROLE TESTER
OLR7 The graduate checks the operation and functionality of the realized code according to test scenarios and improves it based on that feedback from the software developer, analyst, project leader and/or users.
ROLE COMMUNICATOR / TEAM PLAYER
OLR8 The graduate works constructively and actively in a multidisciplinary team and actively participates during consultation moments. The graduate helps find solutions to avoid problems.
OLR9 The graduate communicates and reports efficiently on the work delivered, adapted to the target audience and uses it for this purpose appropriate English jargon.
OLR10 The graduate documents the self-developed applications in an adequate and clear manner using a knowledge database and according to agreements within the organization. The graduate provides qualitative input for the user manuals, reference guides and online resources.
ROLE LIFELONG LEARNING IT PROFESSIONAL
OLR11 The graduate maintains his level of expertise by actively monitoring relevant sustainable IT and social developments to follow up.
OLR12 The graduate is self-critical, develops the necessary self-knowledge and uses this to promote his personal and professional growth.
OLR13 The graduate acts deontologically and sustainably, and takes into account security and privacy guidelines.

Reflection

AIM-reflection on Web Project: CV.

Throughout my Web Project: CV, I’ve developed a lot of core qualities like perseverance, adaptability, and a strong willingness to learn. I faced challenges, especially around sticking to a design and adapting the website to be mobile-responsive, but I pushed through and learned a great deal in the process. One of the biggest lessons I took away is the importance of planning ahead rather than diving straight into things. Early on, I didn’t fully follow the project’s requirements, which led to me overcomplicating the design at times. However, I was able to adjust and refocus, and in the end, I’m proud of the website I created.

Working solo on this project gave me complete creative freedom, which I appreciated, but it also meant I didn’t get external feedback, something I may consider in the future. While I initially struggled with the limitations of using only HTML and CSS, I ultimately learned that I could do a lot more with these tools than I thought. The process made me more confident in building static webpages, and I know I could create something similar much faster now.

Looking back, I see that I could’ve saved time by making decisions about fonts, colors, and images earlier on, instead of focusing so much on the layout first. This would’ve helped me streamline the design process. I also learned how essential it is to have a responsive design, so I focused on using media queries and flexbox containers to ensure the website was user-friendly on mobile devices. I even removed background images that weren’t fitting well on smaller screens, which was a good move for improving the experience across different devices.

Overall, I’m happy with my progress, and I feel much more confident in my ability to build websites. This project has taught me the value of balancing creativity with practical requirements, as well as the importance of planning ahead to save time and avoid unnecessary setbacks. Going forward, I plan to use these insights in my future projects, knowing that a well-thought-out design can make a big difference.
